# Product

## Register

product

## Users

sparks is for people using a mobile app to save meaningful places, organize them into lists, bookmark places to revisit, and explore curated list routes. They may be capturing a place in the moment, planning a visit, browsing saved recommendations, or revisiting a route from a list.

## Product Purpose

sparks is a mobile location-journal and exploration app. A saved location is a spark: a place with personal, social, or route context. The product helps users create sparks, browse a home feed, save and bookmark places, organize sparks into lists, view place details, follow guide routes, and recover safely from empty or deleted content states.

Success means the app feels like a clean, reliable, deployable mobile product translated faithfully from the Figma prototype, with complete flows and interaction-ready screens rather than a class prototype or marketing page.

## Brand Personality

sparks should feel clean, calm, intentional, and personal. The voice is concise and useful. The interface should support the user quietly: clear hierarchy, low clutter, direct actions, and small moments of polish where they help the task.

## Anti-references

Do not treat sparks as a startup landing page, portfolio splash page, or generic marketing site. Avoid generic startup gradients, new decorative graphics, random icon packs, stock or generated imagery, excessive rounded containers, heavy shadows, cluttered map interfaces, default red map pins, placeholder-looking repeated media, and prototype-only controls that do not work.

Do not invent a new visual system. The Figma design system, exported assets, typography, icons, map styling, layout logic, and color palette are the source of truth.

## Design Principles

- Product clarity before decoration: every layout choice should make saving, organizing, or revisiting places easier.
- Figma fidelity is the baseline: reuse existing sparks components, tokens, icons, spacing, typography, and asset treatments.
- Complete flows matter: buttons, states, navigation, map interactions, media choices, and empty or deleted states should behave like a real app.
- Calm density: reduce unnecessary whitespace and clutter while keeping touch targets accessible and content scannable.
- The map and media are product surfaces: they should be reliable, muted, and supportive of spark discovery.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

sparks should use accessible mobile sizing and tap targets, with primary controls at or above 44 by 44 points where possible. Text should remain readable on small screens, avoid overlap, and preserve contrast against the approved palette. Navigation must respect safe areas. Icon-only controls need accessible labels. Empty, loading, error, media fallback, deleted-content, and missing-location states should guide the user without crashing or hiding core actions.
